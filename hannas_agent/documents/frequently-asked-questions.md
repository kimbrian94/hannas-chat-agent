### Do I really need a Night Nurse?
Caring for your baby is important‚Äîbut proper rest for the mother is essential for recovery.

Nighttime feedings and care can significantly disrupt sleep and slow postpartum healing. Accepting help is not a sign of weakness‚Äîit is a choice to prioritize recovery.

Our Night Nurse service takes care of the night so you can rest.

### What does a Night Nurse do?
**Newborn care:**
- Monitor the baby‚Äôs sleep throughout the night
- Feeding (breast, formula, or mixed): bottle feeding and burping
- Diaper changes
- Soothing and putting baby back to sleep (swaddling, calming)
- Care for spit-up or reflux

This allows the mother to get continuous sleep and proper rest.

**Maternal support:**
- Putting the baby back to sleep after nighttime feedings
- For breastfeeding mothers: bringing the baby for feeding and returning them afterward
- Light cleanup of feeding tools (bottle washing and sterilizing)
